Dera Ismail Khan, commonly known as DI Khan, is a historic city located in the southern part of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK), Pakistan. Situated near the Indus River, the city serves as a bridge between the KPK and Punjab provinces. It has a population of over 100,000 people and is known for its cultural diversity, agricultural economy, and peaceful environment. The local language is primarily Saraiki, but Pashto and Urdu are also spoken widely. DI Khan is famous for its dates, mangoes, and sugarcane production. The region has a warm climate, and the fertile land supports farming throughout the year. Historically, the city has been a center of learning and spirituality, with several madrasas, shrines, and historic sites. Educational institutions like Gomal University and the newly established medical colleges attract students from across the country. DI Khan is also strategically important due to its location near South Waziristan and serves as a gateway to tribal areas. The people are known for their hospitality and simple lifestyle. Although it faces challenges like power shortages and lack of infrastructure, DI Khan continues to grow and evolve as an important part of the province.
