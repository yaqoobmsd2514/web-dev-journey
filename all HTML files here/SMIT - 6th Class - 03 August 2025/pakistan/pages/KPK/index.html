Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, commonly known as KPK, is a province located in the northwestern region of Pakistan. It shares a border with Afghanistan and is known for its beautiful valleys, mountains, and rich Pashtun culture. The provincial capital is Peshawar, one of the oldest cities in South Asia, with a history that dates back thousands of years. The majority of the population in KPK speaks Pashto, and the province is famous for its hospitality, bravery, and strong tribal values. KPK is home to stunning tourist destinations like Swat, Malam Jabba, Chitral, and the Kaghan and Naran valleys. The region also includes the Khyber Pass, a historical route that connected Central Asia with the Indian subcontinent. KPK has made significant progress in education, healthcare, and infrastructure over recent years. Its economy is supported by agriculture, mining, and tourism. Despite challenges such as natural disasters and past conflict, the people of KPK are known for their resilience and dedication to peace and development.
