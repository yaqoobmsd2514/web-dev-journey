Rawalpindi, commonly known as Pindi, is a bustling city located in the Punjab province of Pakistan. It lies adjacent to Islamabad, the federal capital, and together they form the twin cities. Rawalpindi is known for its military significance, as it houses the headquarters of the Pakistan Army (GHQ). It is a vibrant city with a rich history, having served as a key garrison town during British rule. It is also an educational hub, home to institutions like Fatima Jinnah Women University and Rawalpindi Medical University. The city has a strong transport network and is connected via the motorway and GT Road. Key landmarks include Ayub National Park, Nawaz Sharif Park, and the historic Murree Road. Rawalpindi is also known for its food culture, with popular street foods and desi cuisine. Despite growing urban challenges, Rawalpindi remains a vital administrative and cultural center of Pakistan.
