Larkana is a prominent city in the Sindh province of Pakistan, located in the northwest of the province. It is known for its political history, educational institutions, and archaeological significance. Larkana is the hometown of former Prime Ministers Zulfikar Ali Bhutto and Benazir Bhutto, making it a stronghold for the Pakistan People‚Äôs Party (PPP). The city is also located near the ancient site of Mohenjo-Daro, one of the most important cities of the Indus Valley Civilization. The main languages spoken here are Sindhi and Urdu. Larkana has a strong agricultural economy, producing crops such as rice, wheat, and sugarcane. The city is home to several colleges and hospitals, serving as a regional hub for education and healthcare. The people of Larkana are known for their hospitality and cultural pride. Traditional Sindhi clothing, music, and food are integral to daily life. The city has good road connectivity and continues to expand with new housing schemes and commercial projects. Despite facing issues such as water supply and urban planning, Larkana remains a city of historical and cultural importance in Pakistan.
