Peshawar is the capital city of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (KPK) and one of the oldest cities in South Asia, with a history dating back over 2,000 years. It is located near the border with Afghanistan and has historically served as a gateway to the Indian subcontinent. The city has seen the rise and fall of many civilizations, including the Greeks, Mughals, and British. Peshawar is predominantly inhabited by Pashtuns, and the Pashto language is widely spoken. It is famous for its unique culture, hospitality, cuisine, and traditional handicrafts. The Qissa Khwani Bazaar (Bazaar of Storytellers) is a historical market known for its rich history and vibrant atmosphere. Despite facing security challenges in the past, the city has made significant progress in urban development, public transport, and tourism. Its resilience, colorful traditions, and historical significance make Peshawar a central figure in the identity of KPK and Pakistan as a whole.
