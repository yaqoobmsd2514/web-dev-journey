Chaghi (or Chagai) is a district in the western part of Balochistan, Pakistan, and is one of the largest districts by area. It gained international fame when Pakistan conducted its first successful nuclear tests here on 28 May 1998 at the Ras Koh Hills. This historic event made Pakistan the first nuclear power in the Islamic world. Chaghi is located near the borders of Iran and Afghanistan and is known for its dry, desert-like terrain. The district has mineral wealth, including deposits of gold, copper, and marble. The Reko Diq area in Chaghi contains one of the world‚Äôs largest undeveloped gold and copper reserves. The local population consists mainly of Baloch tribes, and the main languages are Balochi and Brahui. Due to its remote location, the district faces issues like lack of clean water, healthcare, and education. However, it holds great strategic and economic importance for Pakistan. The mountains of Chaghi, particularly the Ras Koh range, have become symbols of national pride due to the nuclear tests.
